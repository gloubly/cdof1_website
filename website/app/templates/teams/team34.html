Team 34

Testing Landmine Detection Algorithms for Mobile Implementation in Adverse Conditions

Our project focuses on rigorously testing landmine detection algorithms developed in collaboration with partner teams, ensuring their reliability under challenging conditions such as low light, fog, and rain. Using algorithms and datasets provided by these teams, we simulate real-world environments to validate the effectiveness of these detection tools, assessing their performance for integration into a mobile application designed for field use.
To meet the specific requirements for mobile implementation, we conduct our tests on entry-level Android devices with limited processing capacity, including 4 GB RAM and Snapdragon 400 processors. Our evaluation emphasizes critical metrics like true positive and negative rates, response times, and accuracy across varying environmental and terrain conditions. The process involves a comprehensive dataset of approximately 10,000 images that replicate diverse real-world scenarios, allowing us to assess each algorithm’s robustness and responsiveness thoroughly.
By identifying areas for improvement and providing detailed feedback, our work supports the development of reliable landmine detection tools that will be implemented in a mobile application created by another team. This collaborative effort aims to produce a dependable, accessible detection tool that can enhance safety in demining operations worldwide, even under complex environmental conditions.
