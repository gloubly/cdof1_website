Acoustic detection Project

üåç Acoustic Sensors for Humanitarian Demining üé§
Our team is working on acoustic sensors to enhance landmine detection as part of a larger demining initiative. These sensors have the potential to revolutionize how buried mines are identified, offering a safe and innovative approach to this critical challenge.

üî¨ Our Work:

We're focusing on data collection to refine and validate our acoustic sensor technology. Here are our two main strategies:

  1. Simulated Mine Testing: Using 3D-printed mines from our fab lab, we aim to replicate realistic conditions, allowing us to collect acoustic data in controlled environments and analyze the interaction of sound waves with buried objects.
  2. Real-World Data Exploration: We're investigating available datasets from regions heavily affected by landmines, such as Ukraine, Russia, and Cambodia, to enrich our analysis and ensure our approach is applicable in real-life scenarios.

üéØ Why Acoustic Sensors?

  • Although acoustic sensors are commonly used for underwater mine detection, their application for landmines remains relatively underexplored.
  • This technology could prove particularly valuable in muddy terrains, areas covered with snow, or during heavy rainfall, where other methods might be less effective.
  • Non-invasive and adaptable, it offers a scalable solution suitable for diverse and challenging environments.

üë∑ Our Goal:

To develop a precise and efficient acoustic sensor-based detection system, empowering humanitarian organizations with tools to safely and effectively identify landmines.
